I led two teams at Split prior to acquisition:

The first was a team of 5 front end engineers focused on improving our application’s UX. We built the internal component library and drove initiatives to improve performance, accessibility, and developer experience — most notably a complete rebuild of Split’s feature flagging UI, reducing code complexity by 75% and page load time by 50%.

The second was a cross-functional team of 8 engineers responsible for Split’s experimentation functionality. Most notably, I led product-side changes that enabled a significant reduction in processed data usage by shifting the application to read directly from the data warehouse, and built data visualizations that surfaced experimentation results clearly to customers across a platform processing trillions of events.
